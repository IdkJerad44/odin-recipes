Torta de Milanesa

A la chingada estas listo para cocinar, this recipe will have you wanting to speak spanish. So put some latin music to chef it up.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb of skinless, boneless chicken breast
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 egg
  • 1/3 cup of breach crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon of ground chipotle pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried mexican oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon of coarsely ground black pepper
  • 2 or more bolillo rolls, sliced in half lengthwise
  • 3 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon of hot sauce (Valentina or Tapatillo)
  • 2 tablespoons of sunflower seed oil
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 3 slices of tomatos
  • 2 lettuce leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of pickled carrots
  • 1 tablespoon of pickled jalapeno peppers
  • 1 pack of queso fresco

Cooking Steps

  1. Time to preheat yo oven to 250 deg F (120 deg C)
  2. In a sealed Zip-lock place the chicken breast and beat it up, beat it up like babykeem. Get it to about 1/4 inch thick and add cornstarch and shake it up.
  3. Whisk egg in a bowl, dredge the chicken breast through the egg; leave it in the bowl.
  4. In another bowl mix bread crumbs, cilantro, oregano, pepper and salt.
  5. Lift the chicken and place it in the breaded mix, make sure to get it fully covered.
  6. Now grab the bolillo rolls and places them in the oven for about 10 mins
  7. Mix mayo and the hot suace of your choice in a small cointainer
  8. Heat up a skillet to medium heat, cook chicken until brown about 6 mins for each side. Have thermometer in hand and poke it in the center and it should read 165 deg F (74 deg C)
  9. Time to assemble them tortas, spread the spicy mayo on each sid eof the bolillos, add an equal amount of avocado, tomato, lettuce, carrots, jalapenos, and slices of queso fresco.
  10. Complete the tortas with equal amounts of milanesa de pollo and bust open a cervesa and enjoy.
